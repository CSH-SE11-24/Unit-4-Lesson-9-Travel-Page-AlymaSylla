The tea that is used is typically Chinese green tea. The tea is brewed over coals and then poured into another pot. It is poured back and forth and then into glasses. Then again from glass to glass. Typically this happens from a great height above the pot or glass to aerate the tea and create a frothy foam on top.

Tiga dégué, which is rice with peanut sauce, has become the national dish of Mali. The Sarakolé in the Kayes region of Mali are fond of bassi, a couscous made of millet, baobab leaf powder, tomato sauce, milk and peanuts
